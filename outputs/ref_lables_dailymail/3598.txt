horticulturalists have urged people to halt the ' grey sprawl ' in britain which is seeing grass and flowers in front gardens replaced by concrete
more than 7million gardens in britain have now been paved over and the rhs is calling for people to plant hedges , trees and flowers to turn the britain green again - as grassy well kept gardens boost mood the rhs ( rhs ) is calling on people to plant trees , shrubs , climbers , hedges or flowers to attract wildlife , improve air quality and reduce urban temperatures and the risk of flooding
more than 60 per cent said looking at a street of paved - over front gardens saddened them
the rhs says replace paving with plants the rhs has suggested people can transform their concrete front gardens by planting five things ; a tree - intercept intense rain and slow run - off , helping reduce risk of floods a hedge - provide areas of shade a shrub - create extra habitats for wildlife which can promote growing of more plants a climber - cool buildings a perennial plant - reduce pressure on drains which can lead to flash flooding the poll also found that 60 per cent of people worry about paved front gardens and flooding
tips include pulling up a paving stone and planting it up , to creating window boxes and planting trees and climbers in front gardens which can cool buildings
